Onboarding Sequence Fix

Prepared for Miles and the Malohn Capital team, based on the workflow you walked me through.

What you are running

When a client is closed and signs the working capital agreement, GoHighLevel is supposed to kick off onboarding so Jeff can fund them: a confirmation email and reminder text right away, then a second documents sequence shortly after. The intent is solid. The setup underneath it is where the breakdown is.

What is actually breaking

The two sequences are firing off overlapping, ambiguous tags, the immediate one and the 30 minute one are tangled together, and at some point the timing got changed on the wrong workflow. With no clear map of which tag triggers which sequence, it has been mis-firing and the team has been going back and forth on it for two weeks.

  • Ambiguous tags (closed vs close immediate vs close one) make it unclear what triggers what
  • The working capital doc sequence and Jeff's documents sequence got crossed
  • A manual timing change landed on the wrong workflow and quietly broke the flow
  • No documentation, so every fix attempt risks breaking it again

Worth saying clearly: this is a setup problem, not a GoHighLevel limitation. GHL handles an immediate action plus a delayed action without issue when the triggers are clean.

The fix, before and after

Now
One tangled set of tags firing both sequences, with a hand-edited timer that broke the order.
Step 1
Map the intended flow on one page: who should get what, and exactly when, signed off by you and Jeff before anything is touched.
Step 2
Two distinct trigger tags, one per stage. Example: closed-won fires the immediate confirmation, wc-agreement-signed fires the documents sequence. No reused or overlapping tags.
Step 3
Two cleanly named workflows. The delayed one uses a proper 30 minute Wait step inside the workflow, not a manual timer anyone can bump.
Step 4
A test contact runs end to end so we confirm both sequences fire in the right order before it touches a real client.

So it never breaks again

  • A one page automation map: tag, workflow, action, timing, so Jeff and the VA are never guessing
  • Clear naming on every workflow and node, no more "I think this is the one"
  • Notes left inside each workflow explaining what it does and what not to touch
  • Your team stops losing days going back and forth over a sequence
